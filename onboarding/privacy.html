What data we collect

This study collects the following data:

  • Voice recordings (biometric data). Your microphone audio and the voice agent's audio are recorded as a stereo audio file (.webm) for each decision round. Because voice recordings are processed to extract acoustic features such as pitch, loudness, and voice quality measures — going beyond simple transcription — they constitute biometric data within the meaning of GDPR Article 9. This analysis is conducted by the research team; the extracted features contain no personally identifiable information. Voice recordings are potentially identifiable and are therefore treated as pseudonymous personal data throughout.
  • Conversation transcripts. A text transcript of the conversation between you and the voice agent is generated automatically.
  • Survey responses. Your answers to all questionnaire items and slider ratings.
  • Technical metadata. Browser type, screen dimensions, response timing, and connection quality indicators.

Legal basis for processing

The legal basis for processing your personal data is your freely given, informed consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)). Because voice recordings constitute biometric data (a special category under GDPR Art. 9), we additionally rely on your explicit consent to process this special category of data (GDPR Art. 9(2)(a)). You may withdraw this consent at any time (see below).


Third-party data processors

To operate this study, your data is processed by the following third-party services:

  • OpenAI, Inc. (USA). Your voice is transmitted in real time to OpenAI's Realtime API for speech recognition and to power the AI voice agent. Conversation transcripts are also sent to OpenAI's API for automated analysis. OpenAI processes this data under a data processing agreement. See OpenAI's privacy policy.
  • Amazon Web Services (AWS), eu-north-1 (Stockholm, Sweden). Voice recordings are temporarily stored on a private, access-restricted Amazon S3 bucket located in the EU. AWS processes this data under a data processing agreement.

Data retention and storage

Voice recordings will be transferred from Amazon S3 to a password-protected hard drive held at ETH Zurich within two weeks of data collection, after which they will be deleted from AWS. All study data — including recordings, transcripts, and survey responses — will be retained for as long as is necessary for the research and any resulting publications, in accordance with ETH Zurich's data retention policies.

Results of the analyses may be published at an aggregate level in an academic journal. Pseudonymised data (identified only by a random participant code, not by name or voice) may be shared with journal data editors as part of the peer-review process for a resulting publication. We will not share data in any form that could directly identify you.


Interaction with an AI system

During this study you will speak with an AI voice agent powered by OpenAI. You are not speaking with a human. The conversation is processed automatically to extract your stated preference and to analyse vocal characteristics.


Confidentiality and pseudonymisation

Your responses are linked only to a randomly assigned participant code, not to your name or Prolific ID. Voice recordings are potentially identifiable and are therefore not considered fully anonymous. Access to the raw data is restricted to members of the research team and, where required for publication, to journal data editors under equivalent confidentiality obligations.
